\section{Documentation and Operational Readiness}\label{sec:documentation}

The operative question for this phase is whether a competent administrator who has never seen NetFRAME could operate and recover it from the written record alone. The short answer is: for day-to-day operation and most single-service recovery, yes; for a full cold rebuild, mostly, provided they know that \code{CLAUDE.md} and the recent vault AARs supersede the front-facing documents.

\subsection{Strengths}

The documentation culture here is well above the home-lab norm and is a genuine portfolio asset.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \code{CLAUDE.md} is the single most accurate document in the estate and is consistently ahead of every other source (E-12). It carries the authoritative node, IP, service, storage, and safety-note tables and reads as a living operations context file.
  \item A disciplined change record: roughly sixty dated runbooks and AARs under \code{vault/Runbook/}, ten Architecture Decision Records with context, decision, consequences, and alternatives, and formal post-incident RCAs (pve3 e1000e NIC hang, DHCP/Kea outage, backup-verify scheduler, Randy PCIe slot).
  \item Real disaster-recovery material: OPNsense serial-console DR with an age-encrypted config repository, a break-glass credential snapshot on both Ares and Randy that does not depend on the cluster, a node-unreachable triage guide, and a full laptop-recovery runbook (E-21).
  \item The Obsidian vault at \code{Home-Lab/vault/} is declared the authoritative knowledge base and is structured into ADR, Compute, Infrastructure, Networking, and Runbook areas with a master map of content.
  \item Documentation-as-code with CI enforcement: a GitHub Actions job regenerates the topology Mermaid from \code{topology/inventory.yml} and fails the build if the drawing drifts from source.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Weaknesses}

The dominant defect is \sevmed one-directional drift (QL-R19): recent changes land in \code{CLAUDE.md} and the AARs but do not propagate outward to \code{README.md}, \code{topology/}, \code{vault/00 - Homelab MOC.md}, and the \code{docs/} mirror.

\begin{itemize}
  \item Stale clusters D-01 through D-10: Grafana still drawn on pve3 (moved to pve4), Headscale still on pve3 (moved to pve5), Randy CPU still v4/28c (corrected to v3/24c/48t), the DS4246 \code{bulk} pool still 58.2\,T/2-vdev (expanded to 80\,T/3-vdev), OPNsense still 25.7 in about twelve places (live is 25.1.12; 25.7 is the future CARP target), homepage still shown on pve1 CT104 (now pve3 CT106), and the EX3400 \code{native-vlan-id} note still wrong.
  \item Duplicate sources of truth: the same runbook lives in three homes (\code{runbooks/}, \code{docs/}, and \code{vault/Runbook/}) with real content drift in the Randy commissioning runbook and cosmetic drift elsewhere.
  \item Historical documents presented in the present tense: \code{runbooks/EX3400-SSH-Auth-Failure-RCA.md} (references the switch at \code{.10.2}, now the UniFi Dream Router) and \code{homelab-setup.md} need clear "historical/superseded" banners (D-10).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Recommendation}

Adopt a single-source generation model for the \code{docs/} tree so that the vault (or \code{CLAUDE.md}) is the sole authority and \code{docs/} and \code{runbooks/} are generated or reduced to pointers (QL-REC11). The immediate action is to land the D-01 through D-10 documentation reconciliation, which is already staged on the \code{audit/2026-07-22-reconciliation} branch, flowing corrections from \code{CLAUDE.md} outward and keeping the 25.7 reference only in the CARP HA plan. Secret-rotation and certificate-renewal procedures are the two categories most reliant on tribal knowledge and should be written down next.
